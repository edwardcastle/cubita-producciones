Before music, El Micha was a high-performance basketball athlete. That physical discipline shows in his stage presence: stamina, energy and a command of live performance that carries long shows without losing momentum.

His entry into the urban scene came through **Elvis Manuel**, a young Cuban reggaeton reference who died in the Florida Straits between 7 and 8 April 2008 while trying to emigrate. What sets El Micha apart is the breadth of his network. Over his career he has collaborated with top-tier artists including **Gente de Zona, Pitbull, Maluma, Wisin and Daddy Yankee** — a list that speaks both to his versatility and to his standing within Latin music.

He is also one of the most recognisable figures of Cuban reggaeton. Since 2023 he has been involved in a high-profile **"tiradera"** (rap feud) with Chocolate MC — considered one of the originators of the reparto — a clash that has produced several cross songs, including **"Que vaya el Choco"** from El Micha. Beyond the controversy, the episode confirms his relevance and his ability to stay in the conversation.
